# Content Marketing Standards

## Overview

Best practices for creating high-quality content marketing including blog posts, whitepapers, case studies, and long-form content that educates, engages, and converts audiences.

---

## Blog Post Standards

### Structure

**DO**:
- Start with compelling headline (H1) using power words and numbers
- Include introduction that hooks reader within first 2 sentences
- Use descriptive subheadings (H2, H3) every 200-300 words
- Break content into scannable sections with bullet points
- End with clear CTA (call-to-action)

**DON'T**:
- Write generic, clickbait headlines
- Bury the lede (main point) deep in content
- Create walls of text without breaks
- End abruptly without next steps

### Content Quality

- **Length**: 1,500-2,500 words for SEO-optimized posts
- **Readability**: Target 8th-grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid)
- **Originality**: 100% original content, no plagiarism
- **Value**: Provide actionable insights, not fluff
- **Evidence**: Support claims with data, statistics, examples

### Blog Post Template

```markdown
# [Number] [Power Word] [Benefit] [Target Audience]

## Introduction (100-150 words)
- Hook: Compelling question or statistic
- Problem: What pain point does this solve?
- Promise: What will reader learn?

## Main Content
### Subheading 1
- Key point
- Supporting evidence
- Example or case study

### Subheading 2
- Key point
- Supporting evidence
- Example or case study

### Subheading 3
- Key point
- Supporting evidence
- Example or case study

## Conclusion (100-150 words)
- Recap key takeaways
- Reinforce value
- Clear CTA

## CTA
- Download resource
- Subscribe to newsletter
- Book consultation
```

## Long-Form Content (Whitepapers, Guides, eBooks)

### Purpose and Positioning

**Whitepapers**:
- In-depth analysis of industry problem
- Data-driven insights and research
- Thought leadership positioning
- Lead generation tool (gated content)

**Guides**:
- Step-by-step instructional content
- Comprehensive how-to resource
- Educational value
- Authority building

**eBooks**:
- Comprehensive topic coverage
- Multiple chapters/sections
- Visual design elements
- Lead magnet for email capture

### Structure Standards

**Length**:
- Whitepapers: 3,000-5,000 words
- Guides: 2,000-4,000 words
- eBooks: 5,000-10,000 words

**Format**:
- Executive summary (200-300 words)
- Table of contents
- Introduction with problem statement
- Body with clear sections
- Conclusion with recommendations
- About the author/company
- CTA and contact information

### Design Requirements

- Professional cover design
- Consistent typography and branding
- Visual elements (charts, graphs, infographics)
- Page numbers and headers/footers
- Downloadable PDF format
- Mobile-responsive web version

## Case Studies

### Structure (STAR Method)

**Situation**:
- Client background and industry
- Initial challenges and pain points
- Goals and objectives

**Task**:
- Specific problem to solve
- Constraints and requirements
- Success criteria

**Action**:
- Solution implemented
- Process and methodology
- Timeline and resources

**Result**:
- Quantifiable outcomes (metrics, ROI)
- Client testimonial/quote
- Before/after comparison

### Case Study Template

```markdown
# [Client Name]: [Specific Result Achieved]

## Challenge
[Client] faced [specific problem] which resulted in [negative impact].

## Solution
We implemented [specific solution] by [key actions taken].

## Results
- [Metric 1]: [X% improvement]
- [Metric 2]: [Y% increase]
- [Metric 3]: [Z cost savings]

> "[Compelling client quote about results]" - [Name, Title, Company]

## Key Takeaways
1. [Lesson 1]
2. [Lesson 2]
3. [Lesson 3]
```

### Metrics to Include

- Percentage improvements (traffic, conversions, revenue)
- Time savings
- Cost reductions
- ROI calculations
- Customer satisfaction scores

## Storytelling Techniques

### Narrative Arc

1. **Hook**: Grab attention immediately
2. **Context**: Set the scene
3. **Conflict**: Introduce problem/challenge
4. **Climax**: Turning point or solution
5. **Resolution**: Outcome and transformation

### Emotional Connection

**DO**:
- Use relatable characters and scenarios
- Show vulnerability and authenticity
- Appeal to emotions (fear, hope, aspiration)
- Include sensory details
- Use active voice and vivid language

**DON'T**:
- Manipulate emotions dishonestly
- Overpromise or exaggerate
- Use clichés and generic phrases
- Write in passive, corporate jargon

## Content Distribution and Promotion

### Multi-Channel Strategy

**Owned Channels**:
- Company blog
- Email newsletter
- Social media profiles
- YouTube channel

**Earned Channels**:
- Guest posts on industry blogs
- Media coverage and PR
- Influencer mentions
- Backlinks from authoritative sites

**Paid Channels**:
- Promoted social posts
- Content discovery platforms (Outbrain, Taboola)
- Native advertising
- Sponsored newsletters

### Repurposing Content

**From Blog Post**:
- Social media snippets (Twitter threads, LinkedIn posts)
- Infographic summarizing key points
- Video or podcast episode
- Email newsletter feature
- Slide deck for SlideShare

**From Whitepaper**:
- Blog post series (one per chapter)
- Webinar presentation
- Case study extraction
- Social media quote graphics

## Content Calendar and Planning

### Editorial Calendar Components

- **Publication date**: When content goes live
- **Content type**: Blog, whitepaper, case study, etc.
- **Topic/title**: Working title
- **Target keywords**: Primary and secondary SEO keywords
- **Author**: Who's writing it
- **Status**: Ideation, outline, draft, review, published
- **Distribution channels**: Where it will be promoted
- **CTA**: What action should readers take

### Content Cadence

**Recommended Frequency**:
- Blog posts: 2-4 per week
- Long-form content: 1-2 per month
- Case studies: 1 per quarter
- Email newsletters: Weekly or bi-weekly

## Best Practices

### DO

✅ Research audience pain points and interests
✅ Create original, valuable content
✅ Use data and evidence to support claims
✅ Optimize for SEO (keywords, meta tags, internal links)
✅ Include visual elements (images, charts, videos)
✅ Edit and proofread thoroughly
✅ Promote content across multiple channels
✅ Measure performance and iterate

### DON'T

❌ Publish thin, low-value content
❌ Keyword stuff or over-optimize
❌ Plagiarize or duplicate content
❌ Ignore mobile optimization
❌ Forget to include CTAs
❌ Neglect content promotion
❌ Publish without editing
❌ Ignore analytics and feedback

## Metrics and KPIs

### Content Performance Metrics

**Engagement**:
- Page views and unique visitors
- Time on page / Average session duration
- Bounce rate
- Scroll depth
- Social shares and comments

**SEO**:
- Organic search traffic
- Keyword rankings
- Backlinks acquired
- Domain authority impact

**Conversion**:
- Lead generation (form fills, downloads)
- Email signups
- Demo requests
- Sales qualified leads (SQLs)
- Revenue attributed to content

### Tools

- **Analytics**: Google Analytics, Adobe Analytics
- **SEO**: Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz
- **Content Management**: WordPress, HubSpot, Contentful
- **Readability**: Hemingway Editor, Grammarly
- **Design**: Canva, Adobe Creative Suite
